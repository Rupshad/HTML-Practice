Cold Coffee

This classic cold coffee recipe is a standard method for creating a chilled, frothy caffeine beverage at home. It relies on the process of vigorously mixing instant coffee, sugar, and a small amount of water to create a stable foam, which is then combined with cold milk. This technique mimics the texture of professional cafe-style blended coffees without requiring specialized espresso machinery. The result is a balanced, creamy drink that serves as a popular refreshing alternative to traditional hot coffee.

Ingredients

  • Instant Coffee Powder: 2 tablespoons
  • Sugar: 2 tablespoons (adjust to taste)
  • Warm Water: 2 tablespoons
  • Chilled Milk: 1 cup (250ml)
  • Ice Cubes: 4 to 5 cubes
  • Chocolate Syrup: Optional (for garnishing the glass)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Coffee Base: In a small bowl or glass, combine the instant coffee powder, sugar, and warm water.
  2. Create the Froth: Whisk the mixture vigorously using a spoon or a hand frother for 2–3 minutes until the mixture becomes thick, light brown, and creamy.
  3. Prepare the Serving Glass: If using chocolate syrup, drizzle it along the inner sides of a tall glass and place the glass in the freezer for a minute to set.
  4. Combine Ingredients: Pour the chilled milk into a blender. Add the coffee froth and the ice cubes.
  5. Blend: Pulse or blend on high speed for 30–60 seconds until the mixture is uniform and a thick layer of foam forms on top.
  6. Serve: Pour the blended coffee into the prepared glass. Use a spoon to scoop any remaining foam from the blender onto the top of the drink.
